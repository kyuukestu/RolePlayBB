Esther returned her pokemon and warmly caressed both pokeballs. In spite of the odds, they’d done well. There was certainly something to be said about her deliberate injury gambits, but those were the extremes required to even attempt the chasm betwixt herself and trainers with a few years over her.

However, the battle’s conclusion left her hesitant. She hadn’t intended to bring Goomy along, but who could be so cruel as to banish their baby after a show of such bravery and sacrifice? No, no—plans could change. The team she’d take into the tunnels was… yep! A quick toss released all her Pokemon—“Set Up!”—and within a minute or two, Esther and her crew had strung the hammock-tarp between two trees and had fashioned a faux-tent. She slipped inside, a whirlwind of motion as she shed her rancher’s fits for something more sporty and mobile. “Ready!” Although she wanted to carry as many water-types as possible, there were obvious diminishing returns and knowing Water Gun, Nava was at least worth half a water type.
